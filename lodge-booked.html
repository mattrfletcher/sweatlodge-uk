Lodge Booked Information

How to prepare for the lodge

We advise drinking lots of water on the day before the lodge so that you arrive well hydrated. Water will be available during the day too.

We also refraining from any drugs or alcohol for at least 72 hours before the ceremony. A sweat lodge is a very powerful tool to help with alcohol/drug addictions and so it can a wonderful support alongside AA or NA but you need to be clear of these for at least 72 hours.

What to bring

Outdoor clothes and footwear – We spend the day (before, after and in-between the lodge rounds) in a field so please have warm and waterproof clothes as needed according to the season/forecast. We do have a cabin but our ceremony involves lots of outdoor time.

1 set of clothes for the lodge - Loose fitting clothes made of light material are best. Men usually wear shorts and women wear sarongs, with swimwear underneath or long skirts, trousers t-shirts or vest etc). Please ensure you are dressed respectfully (no speedos or see through clothing please - it is a ceremony after all). Also do bear in mind that your clothes will get wet and maybe muddy!

1 or 2 towels and/or a small mat - A dry towel comes in handy during and after the sweats. Also use one, or a pillow or small mat to sit on inside the Lodge if you don’t want to sit directly on the grass.

Flip-flops/sandals – Handy to wear in between the lodge rounds.

Torch - In the winter months when it is dark after the Lodge bring a torch to light your way.

Light food and drink for yourself for the day. Water isavailable on site but we do advise bringing your own water bottle (preferably not plastic) and drinking lots of water.

Food to share with everyone at the meal afterwards. afterwards.It is traditional to have a feast together after the Lodge, so all participants are encouraged to contribute something towards this and please bring your own cup, plate and cutlery.

Ceremonial Things

Pouch of tobacco It is respectful to bring this as an offering. An ordinary sized packet of cigarette tobacco is fine.

Small gift for each of the 2 fire-keepers These individuals work very hard in support of the Sweat Lodge and it is traditional for participants to offer them a gift. A traditional gift would be: food, item, book, picture, crystal, feather, candles, beaded bracelet/necklace, dream-catchers, herbs - whatever comes from the heart is appropriate.

If you have something you would like blessed anything such as a crystal, ring,photograph, amulet etc it can be placed on the altar just outside the Lodge.

Smokers

We ask that all those who smoke are respectful and mindful of others and that it is kept to a minimum; there is a no smoking policy around the Lodge and food areas. If you do smoke please don’t leave your cigarette butts lying around and never throw them (or anything) on the fire, as it is considered a sacred ceremonial fire.

How to Find Us

Ipsden

There is a small lane that goes up to Braziers Park from the A4074 (it is signposted) There is a bus stop here if you are getting the bus from Reading Station.

Trains from London

Trains run from Paddington and Waterloo to Reading. There is a Thames Valley bus service (X39 or X40) from Reading Station to just down the lane from Braziers, about a 30-minute journey, check the bus timetable as they are not that frequent.

There is also a train from London to Goring & Streatley Station – the nearest station to Bra- ziers Park. From here you will need to get a taxi which you may need to book ahead. Or walk (5 miles).

There is more travel information avaliable on the Braziers Park Website.

Overnight Accomodation

You can stay overnight at Braziers retreat centre if they have space, although most rooms are not ensuite. Please contact them on 01491 680221. There are many nice B&B’s close by which are a good option too.

When You Arrive

When you come down the road towards the main house, turn left (there should be a sign pointing to the sweat lodge). There is a place to park on the left (about 1 min drive). Park up and follow the signs to the sweat lodge. Bring all your stuff down to the lodge site. Eventually you will come to the bottom field where you will find us. Here there is a wooden cabin that serves as a kitchen and changing area; there’s also a compost toilet.

We want you to arrive relaxed and peaceful so please leave plenty of time for your journey. We start at 10.30am or as soon as everybody is ready. All phones are turned off on arrival. If you’re running late, don’t worry, just get there as soon as you can.

Above all - have a safe and peaceful journey. To all our relations!
