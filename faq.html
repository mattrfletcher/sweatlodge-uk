Frequently Asked Questions

We have put together some facts to give you more information, please do take time to read this before booking

The numbers vary between 15 - 30 people at each lodge. Booking in advance is essential.

Yes of course. Many people come along by themselves and there are lots of opportunities to get to know others as the day unfolds.

We ask that everyone arrive for 10 am, for a 10.30 start, then the ceremony takes ALL day and most of the evening. One of the lovely things about the lodge is that we follow Indian time, so rather than following the clocks, we go with the flow of the day so we can’t give an exact finish time but it’s usually between 8 - 10pm

A Sweat lodge is a ceremony for purification and healing. There is an opportunity to pray for loved ones whilst inside the lodge but this can be in whatever language you choose.

The ceremony was brought to this land around 2000, by a Blackfoot Sundancer, Elder and Lodge Leader who passed on the tradition over several years, and we now have community members attending the annual Sundance ceremonies in Canada

Our lodge is held in South Oxfordshire - more information will be given on booking

Men usually wear shorts and women usually wear a vest and long skirt or sarong. We will send you a list of what to bring once we’ve received your booking

We are all asked to remain in the lodge during each round, however in an emergency the door will be opened. If you are worried about the heat or feelings of claustrophobia, please speak to one of the lodge leaders during the day as we’ll do what we can to sit you nearer the door.

No drugs or alcohol is allowed on site. We advise refraining from these for at least 72 hours before the ceremony. A sweat lodge is a very powerful tool to help with alcohol/drug addictions and so it can a wonderful support alongside AA or NA.

Tobacco has always been a sacred herb for the Natives and though we don’t smoke or inhale this, we ask you to bring some along as an offering to spirits and for use during the pipe ceremony at the end of the day.

We ask that all those who smoke are respectful and mindful of others and please don’t leave your cigarette butts lying around.
